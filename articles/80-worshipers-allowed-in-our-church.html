80 Worshipers Allowed in Our Church
  • View Larger Image
    • 
    • 

    As of 20 April, St Charles Church (Sw Karol) can admit 80 worshipers into each Mass.  This means our entire English-speaking congregation should be able to attend the English Mass from now on.

    When you come to the church, you will be able to enter through the chapel doors.  That is the south side of the church, or the right side if you are facing the altar.  Someone will count you as you go in. 

    You should check the small table by the gate for a copy of the bulletin with the readings in it.  Bulletins are free; if you pick one up, take it home rather than leaving it in the church.  Bulletins are for two weeks, so you can bring it back the following Sunday depending on the date.

    People are supposed to maintain the standard social distancing of about 2 meters between each person.  When you enter the church, look for places marked with an X in white tape.  These marks indicate where you should sit to maintain the mandated distance from others.  As before, married couples and people sharing living space can sit together, but since you’ll then be taking up a bit more space, try to sit close together and keep as much distance as possible from other people sitting at the nearest X.  

    Face Masks

    Starting 16 April, everyone in Poland is required to wear a face mask while out in public (outdoors, in shops, parks and other public places away from home).  This means that you should wear a mask during Mass.  Of course, you need to lower the mask to under your chin or ‘unhook’ one side of it when you are about to receive Holy Communion.  It is still encouraged to take Communion in the hand, but you will not be forbidden to receive Communion on the tongue.  If you want to kneel to receive Communion on the tongue (probably the ‘safest’ way for the priest to ‘drop’ the host into your mouth without risk of touching your lips or tongue), you can do that by simply kneeling on the lowest step near the altar, off to the side of the queue of people standing for Communion.  (Kneeling for Communion is always permitted following this practice, whether or not there is an epidemic.)

     Confession

    Confession will be available at the main entrance of the church (the vestibule between the outside doors and inner doors).  Fr Marek will be available for confession from 15:30.  To get to confession, enter the church through the chapel door, as you would for Mass.  Then go to the back of the church to the main doors.  There will be a sign indicating if Fr Marek is free for confession or not.  When he’s free, you can go into the vestibule and have confession.

    Remember that Fr Marek needs to be out of the confessional by about 15:55 so he can vest for Mass.  If there’s a line for confession and you’re unable to have confession before Mass, just ask Fr Marek after Mass and he’ll arrange to hear your confession as soon as possible.
