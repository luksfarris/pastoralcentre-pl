Adult Education: Information Meeting 9 May at 17:00

    The Pastoral Centre has recently had a number of requests for lessons in the Catholic Faith.  We are organizing a meeting on Sunday, May 9th, after the English Mass (Mass at 16:00; meeting at 17:00) for people who need some kind of instruction in English.

    So far, we have had requests for

    1. an additional course in Marriage Preparation for people who intend to marry in 2021 (six meetings in six weeks)
    2. the necessary preparation for converting to the Catholic Church (one-two years depending on your background)
    3. a general course of instruction for non-Catholics who are interested in the Catholic Faith, but who do not have a sense of commitment to convert (perhaps because of being married to a Catholic and wanting to know what the spouse believes)
    4. instruction for Catholics who have been baptized but have not yet received the sacraments of first Communion or Confirmation (potentially six months depending on your background and adult knowledge and practice of the Faith).

    All of this instruction is done by one teacher, so an organizational meeting is necessary to figure out how it can be arranged.

    If you or someone you know is an adult English-speaker who is ready to commit to lessons in the Catholic faith, interested people can sign up to attend our organizational meeting on May 9th after the English Mass.

    The last day to sign up to attend this meeting (or to ask for instruction this spring) is MAY 7.  We have many requests and only one (volunteer!) instructor, so people who ask to be added to courses after they have begun cannot be accommodated.

    We will gather outside the south doors of the church (chapel entrance; Krucza street side of the church) at the end of the English Mass (around 16:50, normally) and Fr Marcin will take everyone to the parish room reserved for our meeting.  We need to know how many people will attend the meeting so we can reserve a large enough room to accommodate everyone who is interested.

    If you will be attending the meeting (or attending instruction) with another person (a spouse or fiance/e, for example), please indicate that when you sign up, by adding that person’s name along with your own.  So for example, if you are Jane Doe and you need to be prepared for Confirmation, and your fiance is John Smith and he would like to attend lessons with you (which is highly recommended!), where the form asks for your name, sign this way: Jane Doe (with John Smith).  Then we will know to reserve a large enough room for everyone who will actually attend the meeting or course of instruction.

    At the meeting, we will get to know a bit about you, your situation and needs, and we will try to organize a regular time and place for lessons so that the most people’s needs can be accommodated.  If you do decide to take instruction, it is a commitment of time – depending on the course, up to two years – not only for the instructor, but for the others in your group.  We do not really have the ability to make up missing lessons privately or on an ad-hoc basis for people who skip or miss instruction.  Missing lessons can simply delay your preparation for sacraments or reception into the Church until another course begins and you re-enroll and start over again when you are better able to make a commitment to regular attendance.  Please keep this in mind if you have the goal of preparation for a sacrament or entrance into the Catholic Church.
