Advent Giving

    During Advent, the parish collects food items as well as cleaning and hygiene products for distribution to the poor.

    Food items should be appropriate for long-term storage without refrigeration: dried pasta, rice or other grains; dried soup mixes, beans or legumes; tinned meat, vegetables or fruit; juices in bottles or vacuum packs; dried breakfast food; tea, coffee, sugar; UHT milk, etc. If it does not need refrigeration and it is not frozen, it can be stored and given to the poor as they need it, both during the holidays and in the coming months.

    Most of the food will be distributed to elderly people who can’t afford to buy everything they need. Keep in mind that their tastes may be simple, and they may not be able to do elaborate cooking or baking.

    Cleaning supplies can be for the home or personal hygiene items. Think of the things you use every day: soap, shampoo, toothpaste and toothbrush, shaving cream and razors, toilet paper, hand lotion. Think also of the things you need to clean your house: cleaning cloths, paper towels, window cleaner, floor cleaner, kitchen and bathroom cleaner. A good, general, all-purpose cleaner that will work on many surfaces would be great.

    If you have anything in your house that you know you will not use, you can donate it, as long as it is unused and in a sealed container. (Don’t donate something that you would not want to receive from a stranger and use yourself, please!)

    Or you can buy one or two ‘extras’ next time you are out shopping. Look for special deals (two for the price of one; half-off if you buy three, etc.) and consider giving away the ‘extra’ item to the poor of the parish.

    Items can be left in large baskets that you will see near the doors of the church.

    Because of the ongoing work inside the main church, this week the Masses in the morning and at noon will be held in the downstairs chapel under the church (entrance at the back, behind where the tabernacle is in the church). You can leave your items downstairs during the weekday Mass (call the sacristan’s attention to it), or bring them to Mass on Sunday and leave them in the basket.

    The parish provides for the poor all year around, so while we’re having a special collection now, in Advent, keep in mind that you can always contact Fr Arek or the parish and donate non-perishable food items or cleaning and hygiene products to their pantry for the poor.

    May God bless you generously, for your generosity to His poor ones!
