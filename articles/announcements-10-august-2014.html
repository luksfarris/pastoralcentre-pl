August 15th, Friday, is the Solemnity of the Assumption of the Blessed Virgin Mary into Heaven. It is a Holy Day of Obligation for all Catholics. This means that we are obliged to go to Mass on the Solemnity of the Assumption, just as if it were a Sunday.

If you are travelling, be sure to check ahead and make sure that a Catholic church can be found in the area where you will be travelling, and check for Mass times on the Solemnity of the Assumption.

This is a major Marian feast and is celebrated with special liturgies at Marian shrines around Poland, such as the shrine of the Black Madonna at Jasna Góra in Częstochowa and at Kalwaria Pacławska in eastern Poland (where our Franciscans have their novitiate; if you go to Kalwaria Pacławska for the Solemnity of the Assumption this year, you might meet our Fr Krzysztof, who will be there hearing confessions for the many thousands of pilgrims to the shrine). If you have no plans yet for the holiday weekend, consider making a pilgrimage to one of the many Marian shrines in Poland.

You can read more about the Solemnity of the Assumption of the Blessed Virgin Mary on the Catholic Answers website.
