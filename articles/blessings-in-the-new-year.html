Blessings in the New Year!

    We wish you God’s grace and blessing, His leadership and friendship, and that His peace may reign in your hearts and homes throughout the New Year!

    We begin the New Year with several important feasts connected with the Nativity of the Lord.

    On Sunday, December 30th, we celebrate the Feast of the Holy Family of Jesus, Mary and Joseph.  It’s a day to reflect on the beauty of the Holy Family, and to entrust our own families to heart of Jesus, and the intercession of Mary and Joseph.  

    Tuesday, January 1 is the Solemnity of Mary, the Mother of God. It is a Holy Day of Obligation, and our English Mass is at 4PM as usual.  If you are travelling, be sure to find a Catholic Church and attend Mass on January 1.

    Sunday, January 6 is the Feast of the Epiphany of the Lord, when Jesus was acknowledged as King of Kings by the three Wise Men.  It’s a good time to reflect on who is ‘king’ in our lives? Who makes the rules – Jesus, or us? Is there some area in our life that we do not want to submit to his kingship?

    Sunday, 13 January, we celebrate the Baptism of the Lord, commemorating when Jesus – although he was without sin – submitted to Baptism to initiate the Sacrament of Baptism for us, so we might be reconciled with our heavenly Father.  Do you remember the promises that were made at your Baptism?  Do you still live by those promises?

    Keep in mind our social events after Masses in January:

    13th January – Social Hour

    20th January – Board-Games Evening

    27th January – Bible Study (chapters 5 and 6 of the Gospel of John).

    We hope to see you at Mass and at our social events.

    Happy New Year!
