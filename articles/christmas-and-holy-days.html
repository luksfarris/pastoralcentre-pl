    English-language Masses will be at 4PM (16:00) on Christmas, December 25th; the Solemnity of Mary, the Mother of God, January 1st; and the Epiphany of the Lord, January 6th.

    Catholics are obliged to attend Mass on the Holy Days of Obligation in the country where they are staying temporarily or travelling, rather than the traditional Holy Days in their home country.  

    Holy Days may be different from country to country, so if you are travelling, you need to look up the Holy Days of Obligation in the countries you will be visiting, and make arrangements to attend Mass on those days.  Missing Mass on a Holy Day of Obligation or a Sunday when it is possible for you to attend is a mortal sin.  This means that if you miss a Sunday or Holy Day Mass out of laziness or a preference for other activities (sight-seeing, shopping, excursions, and so on), unnecessary travel or work, poor planning that could have been avoided, etc., you need to go to confession before receiving Holy Communion again.

    You can find a list of Holy Days of Obligation in many countries on Wikipedia.
