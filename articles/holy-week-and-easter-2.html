Holy Week and Easter

    If you’re staying in Wrocław – and even if you’re not – here’s everything you need to know about Holy Week events and Easter liturgies in our parish. Much of this information should apply to every parish in Poland and most Catholic parishes in the world.

    Confessions in Holy Week

    Monday: 6:30-8:30; 11:45-13:00; 17:30-18:45
    Tuesday: 6:30-8:30; 11:45-13:00; 17:30-24:00
    Wednesday: 6:30-8:30; 11:45-13:00; 17:30-18:45
    Thursday: 6:30-9:00; 16:30-17:45
    Friday: 6:30-9:00; 16:30-17:45

    It is unlikely that Fr Arek already knows when he will have confession duty during Holy Week, so he might not be able to tell you when you will find him in a confessional. Lines will be very long at the scheduled times during Holy Week. Bear in mind that the priests have many hours in the confessional as well as their other duties – Masses, teaching, working in the hospital, parish duties, preparation of the church for Easter, preparation for special liturgies – and be considerate of Fr Arek’s busyness in Holy Week if you ask for an appointment for confession. Be prepared to work around his free time, rather than insisting on a particular time that suits you best.

    Holy Thursday: 18 April, 2019

    • Mass: 18:00 (Arrive 60 minutes early for your choice of seats; 45-30 minutes early just to get a seat. This advice applies to just about every parish church in the city. )
    • Adoration of the Blessed Sacrament until 22:00

    Good Friday: 19 April, 2019

    • Adoration of the Blessed Sacrament from 6:30
    • Liturgy of the Hours: 8:00
    • Stations of the Cross: 15:00
    • 17:30: Chaplet and Novena of Divine Mercy, Day 1
    • Good Friday Liturgy (with Holy Communion): 18:00 (Arrive 60 minutes early for your choice of seats; 45-30 minutes early just to get a seat.)
    • Adoration of the Blessed Sacrament at the ‘tomb’ of Christ: until 23:00
    • Stations of the Cross: 22:00 (Bring a candle. These stations are done while walking around outside the church. Dress appropriately for the weather and for walking on uneven ground. Candles with plastic ‘cups’ to shield the flame are less likely to blow out and also reduce the risk of dripping wax and accidental burns. Parish shops and religious shops usually have cups that slide over candles, or make your own using a plastic drinking cup.)

    Holy Saturday: 20 April, 2019

    • Adoration of the Blessed Sacrament at the ‘ tomb’ of Christ: from 6:30
    • Liturgy of the Hours: 8:00
    • Blessing of Easter food baskets: from 10:00 to 16:00, more or less continuously as crowds dictate (These are baskets filled with traditional Polish Easter foods and decorated in a traditional way, not children’s baskets with chocolate eggs and bunnies. Please respect the tradition if you bring a basket.)
    • 16:00-19:00: Church closed for cleaning

    Easter Vigil Liturgy (20 April)

    • 21:30: Begins outside the Church; please bring a candle (To be honest, if you want a seat, come 45-60 minutes early and don’t go outside unless you really want to see them light the fire and prepare the Paschal candle or you’ll stand for the whole liturgy.)
      This is a long service, especially for young children, who often fall asleep or complain of the cold in the church. If you are bringing children, plan ahead with a light blanket and make sure that you arrive early enough for them to get a seat. Children are less likely to become bored and ‘challenging’ if you sit close to the front where they can see what is going on, and explain the action and read the readings in your own language while the Polish is being read. It’s also a good idea to find out where the toilets are in the church (either through the sacristy and downstairs or outside, enter by the sacristy door, and go downstairs) if you have young children who may need to go during the liturgy.

    Easter Sunday: 21 April, 2019

    • English Mass at 16:00
      Confession as usual before Mass at 15:30.

    Easter Monday is a holiday, and Poles go to Mass on this day. It is not a Holy Day of obligation, so there will not be a Mass in English. However, everyone is encouraged to attend Mass in Polish on the usual Sunday schedule of Masses.
