Making a Spiritual Communion

    • 
    • 

    At this time when most Catholics simply cannot get into a church to attend Holy Mass, it is more urgent than ever that we all know how to make a Spiritual Communion.

    Making a Spiritual Communion is something you can do every day.  It is not a substitute for going to Mass: we are obliged to attend Mass on Sundays and Holy Days of Obligation, and we sin if we deliberately fail to worship God in the liturgy on those days, or if we fail to attend Mass out of laziness or choosing some other activity over God.

    However, in these times of quarantine, most Catholics have to stay home from Sunday Mass – whether they want to or not – because parishes are not allowed to let all the faithful into the church for Mass.

    In this extraordinary time we must consider making a Spiritual Communion every day, either alone or with our family.  And certainly we should make a Spiritual Communion any time we watch Mass on television, the Internet or listen to it on the radio because we are legitimately unable to attend Holy Mass on Sunday or a Holy Day.

    Preparing to Make a Spiritual Communion

    Make sure that you and your home are quiet and peaceful.  Remember that we gather quietly in church before Mass, and that candles are lit.  Because of the many distractions in our homes, it is appropriate and useful to quiet yourself, turn off all phones and electronics, and ask family members to maintain silence for 15 minutes before participating in Mass online, on television or on the radio.  Lighting candles in front of a crucifix and sitting quietly can help you and your family recollect yourselves for entering into Mass spiritually.  Quieting yourself and your environment, lighting a candle and gazing at the crucifix are also good practices for making a Spiritual Communion at any time.

    The Steps of Making a Spiritual Communion

    Here’s how to make a Spiritual Communion.  If you are using this while watching or listening to Mass in your home, use point 5 to make your Spiritual Communion during the distribution of Communion in Mass.

    1. Make the Sign of the Cross.

    2. Make a brief examination of conscience. The United States Conference of Catholic Bishops has a good one that you can print out or download onto your phone and use before Mass. 

    3. Meditate briefly on the prayers and readings of the Mass for the day.

    a. Collect, Prayer over the Offerings, Prayer after Communion. You can read the prayers for the 5th Sunday in Lent on liturgies.net and use the same site to find these prayers for any Sunday in the year. 

    b. First Reading, Responsorial Psalm, Second Reading (on Sundays),  Gospel. You can find the readings for March 29 on the USCCB website.  The same site has a calendar where you can click on any day and find the readings for that day.

    c. One of the Eucharistic Prayers (Eucharistic Prayer II is found here.) 

    4. Make acts of faith, hope, and love as you prepare to receive the graces God wills to give.

    Act of Faith

    O my God, I firmly believe that you are one God in three divine Persons, Father, Son, and Holy Spirit. I believe that your divine Son became man and died for our sins and that he will come to judge the living and the dead. I believe these and all the truths which the Holy Catholic Church teaches because you have revealed them, who are eternal truth and wisdom, who can neither deceive nor be deceived. In this faith I intend to live and die. Amen.

    Act of Hope

    O Lord God, I hope by your grace for the pardon of all my sins, and after life here to gain eternal happiness because you have promised it, who are infinitely powerful, faithful, kind, and merciful. In this hope I intend to live and die. Amen.

    Act of Love

    O Lord God, I love you above all things and I love my neighbor for your sake because you are the highest, infinite and perfect good, worthy of all my love. In this love I intend to live and die. Amen.

    5. Make an Act of Spiritual Communion:

    My Jesus, I believe that you are present in the Most Holy Sacrament. I love you above all things, and I desire to receive you into my soul. Since I cannot at this moment receive you sacramentally, come at least spiritually into my heart. I embrace you as if you were already there and unite myself wholly to you. Never permit me to be separated from you. Amen.

    6. Make an Act of Thanksgiving for graces received:

    From the depths of my heart I thank you, dear Lord, for your infinite kindness in coming to me. How good you are to me! With your most holy Mother and all the angels, I praise your mercy and generosity toward me, a poor sinner. I thank you for nourishing my soul with your Sacred Body and Precious Blood. I will try to show my gratitude to you in the Sacrament of your love, by obedience to your holy commandments, by fidelity to my duties, by kindness to my neighbor and by an earnest endeavor to become like you in my daily conduct.  Amen.
