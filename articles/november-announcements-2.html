    We begin November with a Holy Day of Obligation: All Saints’ Day. Our English Mass will be at 4PM. If you’re travelling on All Saints’ Day, be sure to plan ahead so that you know where and when you can go to Mass. Missing Mass on a Holy Day of Obligation is a serious sin – like skipping Mass on Sunday. So make time for God on the Holy Day.

    If you’re staying in Wroclaw and would like to spend the Holy Day the way Poles do, visit a cemetery near you. Poles light thousands of candles on the graves of their relatives (dusk or after dark is when the cemetery looks most beautiful in the candlelight). People clean gravestones, place flowers, and pray for the repose of the souls of their departed relatives. There are also rosary processions in the cemeteries.

    2. It is the custom in Poland to pray for the dead in November. In all churches, there are sheets of paper on which people write the names of their departed family, friends and benefactors. (Often, the form will ask you to write down the name of the street where you live, so the priests can announce which ‘streets’ will be prayed for on which day.) The names of the faithful departed are read out before Masses, and the repose of their souls is included in the prayers of the Mass.

    For English-speakers who attend our English Mass, we have provided yellow papers for you to write down the names of your dead. We ask you to PRINT NEATLY and to write only the first name (personal name) of the people you want us to pray for.

    You can put your yellow paper in the collection basket at Mass, or you can give it to the sacristan in the sacristy. Only the YELLOW papers will be set aside for prayer in the English Mass. We’ll read the names at the last Mass this month, 25 November.

    3. Soon, we will be starting a class for adult speakers of English who have not yet had the sacrament of Confirmation. The class will meet during the week, after work hours and will last 12-15 weeks (depending on holidays and other interruptions). If you need the sacrament of Confirmation, contact us through this website or go to the sacristy after Mass and let Fr Arek know that you need Confirmation.

    When you’ve finished the classes, you’ll be ready to have Confirmation in your home country (we can give you a document to show to your priest) or here in Poland (it’s possible in the Cathedral in Wrocław).

    4. Don’t miss our upcoming events after Masses in November:
    Social Hour: November 11
    Games Evening: November 18
    Bible Study (Gospel of Mark): November 25
