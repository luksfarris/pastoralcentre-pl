Palm Sunday

    April 14 is Palm Sunday. If you are new to Poland, you may be surprised that the ‘palms’ you should bring to Mass are not palm branches, but dried flower arrangements. You can find Polish ‘palms’ almost everywhere at this time of year. Try supermarkets, flower shops and kiosks that sell magazines. Once you know what you are looking for, you’ll find the palms seemingly everywhere you look.

    At Mass on Palm Sunday, we will begin with blessing of the palms and a procession. Everyone attending Mass will be asked to gather at the back of the church (main doors), with your palms (and even if you don’t have a palm with you). Fr Arek will bless the palms (or you, if you have no palm) with holy water, and will lead the procession up the main aisle to the altar. The congregation follows the priest and altar-servers, and you simply enter your pew when the procession reaches the place where you want to sit.

    During Palm Sunday Mass, it’s important not only to bring a palm, but to pick up a Bulletin when you come into the church. You can find the Bulletins on a small table near the south (chapel) entrance or on a chair near the pews at the back of the church when you come in the main entrance. The Bulletins have the readings for the Mass, as well as articles that you can read to learn more about your Catholic faith. You are welcome to take the Bulletin home with you, and everyone is encouraged to use the Bulletin during Mass.

    On Palm Sunday, however, there is a special reason for using the Bulletin: the congregation will be expected to read aloud the part of the ‘Crowd’ in the Passion narrative for Palm Sunday. The congregation’s parts are marked in bold print, and prefaced with the word ‘All.’ Everyone should read those parts aloud during the Gospel on Palm Sunday.
