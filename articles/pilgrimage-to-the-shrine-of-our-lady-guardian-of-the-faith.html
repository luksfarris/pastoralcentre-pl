Pilgrimage to the Shrine of Our Lady, Guardian of the Faith
  • View Larger Image

    The Pastoral Centre for English-speakers invites you on a pilgrimage to the Shrine of Our Lady, Guardian of the Faith, in Bardo Śląskie (78 km south of Wrocław), on Saturday, May 15th. Please join us!

    At the shrine of Our Lady we will pray the rosary while walking the Stations of the Holy Rosary , then celebrate Holy Mass. We also will have time before the Blessed Sacrament to pray for an increase in faith: for ourselves, our families, Poland, our home countries and the whole world. During Adoration, we will pray the Litany of Loreto.

    During Mass, there will be a collection taken up to support the shrine.

    Confession will be available with a priest who speaks English (after 10 years in the US).

    It will be possible to buy a hot meal (soup and main dish) at the Pilgrimage House (cost: 30 zł.). Or you can bring your own food from home.

    There will be free time for prayer, or – if you wish – to climb to the top of a hill and visit the Chapel of the Mournful Mother of God.

    If you want to come, please go to our sign-up sheet .  We will need to know how many people wish to reserve a meal at the shrine (30zl), and we’ll need your contact information so we can count heads at the station and make sure that everyone is present before we leave.

    TICKETS:
    We will travel to Bardo Śląskie by train (the name of the train is Koleje Dolnośląskie, abbreviated as KD) leaving at 8:26 and arriving in Bardo Śląskie at 9:47.
    We will return to Wroclaw on the same train leaving Bardo Śląskie at 17:09 and arriving in Wroclaw at 18:28.

    The cost of the ticket is 20.10 zl one-way (total cost of the return ticket: 40.20 zl).

    You can buy your tickets in the main ticket hall at the station or online here.

    Total cost (tickets and meal): 70.20 zl (Also add in whatever you might want to donate to the shrine during the collection at Mass.)

    Meeting time: 8:00

    Meeting place: Wrocław Główny, the Pilsudskiego street entrance, outside the main doors, under the portico (not inside the train station). This covered area is small enough that we should easily be able to see other members of the group gathered together.

    We look forward to seeing you there!
