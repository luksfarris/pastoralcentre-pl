Receiving a Plenary Indulgence during the Year of Faith

On 14 September 2012, the Apostolic Penitentiary decreed that during the Year of Faith (October 11, 2012 – November 24, 2013), it would be possible for the faithful to receive a plenary indulgence from temporal punishment for their sins (the indulgence may also be offered for the souls of the faithful departed).

In order to receive the indulgence, the usual conditions apply:

1. Making a full sacramental confession

2. Receiving Holy Communion

3. Praying for the intentions of the Holy Father.

In addition, for this indulgence, the faithful must fulfil one of the following conditions:

1. Attending three lectures during Mass in a Parish Mission on any subject; or three lectures on the Documents of Vatican II or the Catechism of the Catholic Church, in a church or other designated place.

2. Making a pilgrimage to a Papal Basilica, the Catacombs or a Christian sanctuary designated by the Bishop of the Diocese during the Year of Faith and taking part in a liturgical worship service there; or pray or meditate, ending with the Our Father, the Creed, and a prayer invoking the Blessed Virgin or another saint. This can be done on any day in the Year of Faith.

St Charles Borromeo parish is a ‘designated sanctuary’ because of the Shrine of Our Lady of Grace. This means that if we make a good confession, receive communion and pray for the Holy Father as well as participating in some kind of liturgical worship (Mass, organized worship like a litany or other worship service) in St Charles Church on any day during the Year of Faith, we gain the indulgence.

Or if we make a good confession, receive communion, and pray for the Holy Father as well as praying or meditating in the church, ending our prayer/meditation with the Our Father, the Creed and a prayer invoking the Blessed Virgin or another saint, we can gain the indulgence.

According to the Decree, the Archdiocese of Wrocław has appointed the following churches where condition number 2 applies:

WROCLAW:

Cathedral of St. John the Baptist and the Minor Basilica of St. Elizabeth

And the Shrines:

  • Our Lady of Grace (church of St. Charles Borromeo)
  • Our Lady of Mercy (Church under the Protection of St Joseph)
  • Mary, Our Lady of Consolation (church of St. Clement)
  • Our Lady of the Rosary (Church of the Saint Wociech [Adalbert])
  • Our Lady of Victories (Church St. Mary on the Sand);

OLEŚNICA:

Minor Basilica of St. John the Apostle and Evangelist

TRZEBNICA:

Minor Basilica of Sts Bartłomiej and Jadwiga;

ZIEBICE:

Minor Basilica of St Jerzy

3. Taking part in the solemn celebration of the Eucharist or the Liturgy of the Hours, including praying the creed, in any holy place on a day established by the Diocesan Bishop for the Year of Faith.

This means that on the days listed below, make a solemn confession, pray for the Holy Father, go to Mass and receive communion you will receive the indulgence.

Or, if you make your confession, receive communion and pray for the Holy Father as well as praying the Liturgy of the Hours and reciting the creed, you will receive the indulgence.

4. When you return to the church in which you were baptized, on any day of your choosing within the Year of Faith, and there renew your baptismal promises.

The last day to receive the indulgence is the

Solemnity of Christ the King,

November 24.
