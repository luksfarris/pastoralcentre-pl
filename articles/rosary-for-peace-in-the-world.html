PENDING: Rosary for Peace in the World
  • View Larger Image
    • 
    • 

    NOTE: While we are still planning this event, because of the coronavirus restrictions recently implemented in the parish, we may have to cancel it at short notice. Please pray that the crisis with the epidemic will not prevent us imploring Our Lady to bring peace to the world!

    On Thursday evening, October 29, 2020, at 19:15 the Pastoral Centre will be hosting an International Rosary for Peace in the parish of St Charles Borromeo (ul Krucza), Wroclaw.  The rosary will be prayed after the Mass in Polish (which begins at 18:00).  We warmly welcome you to come and pray the Rosary together with us in your own native language.

    Here’s how it will work: Volunteers will pray the prayers of the Rosary by beginning each prayer in their own language; the congregation will finish each prayer in their own languages.  

    So, for example, the leader might pray, ‘Hail Mary, full of grace, the Lord is with thee.  Blessed art thou among women and blessed is the fruit of thy womb, Jesus.’ Then the rest of the assembly, following on their rosaries, will conclude in their own languages: ‘Holy Mary, Mother of God, pray for us sinners, now and at the hour of our death.  Amen.’ 

    We will do the same for the Lord’s Prayer and the Glory Be.

    Between the decades of the Rosary, we will sing the Taizé Canon ‘Da pacem, Domine‘ (Lord, give us peace) in Latin.

    So far, people have expressed willingness to lead a prayer or prayers in the following languages: English, Polish, French, Spanish, German, Latin, Esperanto, Irish, Italian, Eastern Syriac, Western Syriac, Russian and Ukrainian.  The people who will lead the prayers can be from any country (for example, French-speakers from France, Togo, Canada, Cameroon, Belgium…).  We would like to have multiple people from around the world leading the prayers in their own languages.  Mary is the Mother of All Christians; your mother-tongue is our Mother’s mother-tongue!  So we welcome you to contact us if you want to pray one or more prayers of the Rosary at this event.

    You can use our contact form to get in touch.  Or if you attend the Mass, go to the sacristy after Mass and put your name on the list next Sunday, October 18.  You can also sign up online by following this link.

    We have to organize the logistics of how we will pray  by Sunday, 25 October at the latest, so if you’re interested, contact us by the 18th. You are welcome to invite your friends from any language group, even if they don’t speak English or Polish.  If you are there to help them step forward and lead a prayer in their own language, they are most welcome!
