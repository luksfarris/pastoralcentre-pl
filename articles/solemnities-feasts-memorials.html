Throughout the liturgical year, the Church celebrates a variety of solemnities, feasts and memorials.

The word “feast” is a general term used to denote festivals that commemorate a teaching or event of religious importance or a particular saint. As the Church celebrates the mystery of Christ throughout the liturgical year, Mary, the Mother of God is especially honored, as are the saints and martyrs, all of whom are recommended to us as examples.

Solemnities are the most important feasts of the Church. The celebration of a solemnity begins with the first vespers of the preceding day. Thus several solemnities have their own vigil Mass, which is celebrated in the evening on the day before the solemnity itself. The Nativity of the Lord, for example, has its Vigil Mass – Christmas Eve “Midnight Mass” (in Polish, Wigilia). Other solemnities include the Epiphany, the Ascension, and Pentecost. The celebration of the Solemnities of Easter and Christmas are so important that they continue for eight days.

Some solemnities are Holy Days of Obligation. These vary from country to country.  In Poland, the Holy Days of Obligation are:

  • Mary, the Mother of God (January 1)
  • Epiphany (January 6)
  • Corpus Christi (Thursday after Trinity Sunday)
  • The Assumption of the Blessed Virgin Mary into Heaven (August 15)
  • All Saints’ Day (November 1)
  • Christmas Day (December 25)

(These feasts of the Church are also public holidays in Poland.)

Feasts honour a title (e.g., Christ the King) or a mystery of the Lord (e.g., Corpus Christi – the Body and Blood of Christ); or they honour a title or mystery of Our Lady.  Feasts also honour saints of particular importance (for example, Apostles or Evangelists) or historical significance. Feasts are usually celebrated within the limits of the natural day on which they fall; ordinarily they have no vigil Mass.

Memorials usually honour saints, but may also celebrate some aspect of the Lord Jesus or Our Lady, such as the memorial of the Holy Name of Jesus or the memorial of the Immaculate Heart of Mary. Like feasts, memorials are celebrated within one 24-hour day and have no vigil Mass.
